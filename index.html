I am a PhD Candidate and a Tutor at the School of Business and Economics at Maastricht University. Together with Till Moritz Vater, I am co-organizing the Interdisciplinary Brown Bag Seminar in Behavioral Sciences. Additionally, I serve as a pro bono Adjunct Professor of Economics at the School of Science at the Brīvā Universitāte (Svobodny University).

My research interests are Behavioural and Experimental Economics, (Behavioural) Game Theory, and Political Economy.

In the 2025–2026 academic year, I will be a U.S.–RAR Fellow and a Malyi Visiting Fellow at the University of Chicago.

Furthermore, I am affiliated with the Berkeley Initiative for Transparency in the Social Sciences (UC Berkeley), the Institute for Replication, and Dissernet.

Previously, I worked at Shaninka University, European University at St. Petersburg, Harvard University, Utrecht University, the University of Vienna, HSE University, and Transparency International Russia.

Selected Publications

2023

  1. [Psychology]
    Book Review of ’The Power Of Experiments: Decision Making In A Data-Driven World’ by Michael Luca & Max H. Bazerman.
    Egor Bronnikov
    Personnel Psychology,  2023,  vol. 76,  no. 4, pp. 1219-1221
    Abstract      HTML            PDF      

    What should a letter from the National Tax Authority include to maximize tax payments? How can discrimination be minimized in online markets? Is it possible that a brief two-line notification to students’ parents can result in a substantial reduction in class absenteeism? This review essay discusses the new book by Luca & Bazerman (2021) which provides answers to these and numerous other questions.

  2. [Political Science]
    Book Review of ’Voicing Politics: How Language Shapes Public Opinion’ by Efrén Pérez & Margit Tavits.
    Egor Bronnikov
    Democratization,  2023,  vol. 30,  no. 8, pp. 1628-1630
    Abstract      HTML            PDF      

    Can languages influence political preferences? How can the influence of language be accurately separated from culture? Does language shape public opinion? And why is language important for democracy? This paper reviews a recent book by Pérez and Tavits (2022) that sheds light on these inquiries.

  3. [Economics]
    Book Review of ’The Voltage Effect: How to Make Good Ideas Great and Great Ideas Scale’ by John List.
    Egor Bronnikov
    Public Choice,  2023,  vol. 194, pp. 229-230
    Abstract      HTML            PDF      

    Can every idea be scaled? Is there an inherent factor in a business or public policy proposal that inherently makes it a winning concept, or is everything contingent on its execution? This review explores the new book by List (2022), which delves into these questions.
